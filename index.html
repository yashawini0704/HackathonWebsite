Objective

We are launching DesiCrew's first company-wide AI hackathon. It is designed to help Crewmates across all DC centres explore and apply AI tools (without any coding) to solve real workplace problems. The objective is to reduce manual effort, save time, and improve internal processes.

This initiative is open to all DC crewmates and encourages collaboration across teams and centres. It will focus not just on final outputs but on consistent participation, learning, and problem-solving using AI-based tools.

Participation

  • Team size: 3 to 5 members
  • Each team must include one DNext Leader as a full participant

Judging Criteria

  • Clarity of problem and relevance
  • Functionality of the solution
  • Ease of use by other teams
  • Demonstrated effort and learning during the process

Post Hackathon

  • Winning tools will be refined with support from Project Vikram
  • Functional tools will be implemented across teams where applicable
  • All working solutions will be showcased in DC's AI Solutions Library
  • Select tools and stories will be featured on Vikram's & DC's website and social media

Hackathon Phases

Awareness Sessions & Workshops

During this phase Team Vikram & DTouch will conduct 3-6 awareness sessions & workshops via DC LIVE to:

  • Explain the purpose and structure of the hackathon
  • Introduce AI tools to use from the following list: ChatGPT, Softr, Firebase Studio, Make, Claude, Gemini
  • Share simple use cases and how to create basic prototypes, along with doubt clearing sessions
Important: Teams must attend at least one session to be eligible for shortlisting.

Submission Requirements

Each team must submit the following on or before July 15th:

  • Full team details, including names and employee id for each team member and team captain details
  • Problem statement
  • Proposed solution using AI tools
  • Tools planned for use from the list of tools provided
  • Sample prototype (can be a Word document, PPT, diagram, or short video)

Evaluation Process

A jury comprising members from Project Vikram and other internal & external leaders will shortlist 15 teams to proceed to the next phase.

Results Announcement

Publishing of shortlisted teams (July 21st)

Development Period

Shortlisted teams will build and submit a working version (a demo video) of their proposed tool on or before July 28th.

Recommended: Teams are encouraged to spend at least one hour per day on this.

Support Available

Teams may seek clarification or basic support from the Project Vikram team during the week.
